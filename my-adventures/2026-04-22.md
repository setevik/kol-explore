### Wiki Research on Pool Skill (logging to mechanics)
User asked me to check the wiki. Summary of what I found:
- **Pool cue** (equipped): +3 Pool Skill
- **Chalky Hand** (hand chalk use): +3 Pool Skill (decays ~1 per adv)
- **Practice at the other table**: +1 or +2 Pool Skill per practice, permanent — this is why my base skill climbed to 10 without any buffs
- **Drunkenness**: +1 per point up to 10 (so max +10 at 10 drunk). **Each drunkenness point past 10 gives -2.** Do NOT overshoot 10.
- **Ghost target**: rolls a secret number **14–18**. Need Pool Skill ≥ roll to win. So 18 is "guaranteed win", 16 is "win often."

My math: practiced (+10) + cue (+3) + Chalky Hand (+3) + 10 drunk (+10) = **~26 Pool Skill** at peak. Overkill.

### What Worked
- **Disguise + perfume + cake** — the Goblin King's answer is 100% prep-based, not stat-based. Once I had the right items, the fight was trivial with Entangling + Shurikens.
- **Pool Skill stacking** — practice is free (well, 1 adv each) and permanent. Get pool skill to 10+ via practice, then drink + chalk for the final push.
- **Hand chalk drops from chalkdust wraiths** — self-sustaining supply for Billiards Room. Always carry 2-3 chalks.
- **The Goblin King Crown** (70 power hat) is a major upgrade. Probably permanent endgame-ish hat until ascension.

### What Didn't Work
- **Cyrpt without Smothers** — 30 spooky damage per hit tanks through 40 HP fast. Need to plan the blessing each day.
- **"Bottle of rum" for drunkenness** — 3 drunk each, so 4 of them = 12 drunk (overshoot). Plan booze more carefully: 3 × 3-drunk bottles + 1 × 1-drunk Imp Ale = exactly 10.
- **Spaghetti Spear vs chalkdust wraiths** — 1 dmg per spear. Use firecrackers or Shurikens.
