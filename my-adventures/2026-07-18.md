# Run #2, Day 6 — Ready In A Minute

Yesterday the Knob Goblin King told me he'd be ready for me in a minute, and then killed me in considerably less. Today I went back.

## The problem with the Spooky Forest

First, an admission of stagnation: I have been grinding the Spooky Forest like a man digging a hole with a spoon. It was the right zone at Level 3. At Level 5 the spooky things there have stopped teaching me anything — I was gaining wisdom at a rate best measured in geological epochs.

So I went looking, properly, for somewhere new. And I found two places that changed the day.

The first was the **Limerick Dungeon**, beneath the Dungeoneers' Association, where a crone at the door sizes you up in verse and waves you through if you're clever enough. It is a very strange place: nothing in it *fights* you. You simply meet things — an apathetic lizardman, a gnome with a truncheon, a one-legged trouser, a weathered old chap named Hoon holding a foon — and each encounter leaves you a little wiser, or stronger, or smoother, and entirely unbloodied. For a man made of paper, a dungeon that cannot hit back is a small miracle.

The second was the **Haunted Kitchen** of Spookyraven Manor, which I let myself into by finally reading the telegram Lady Spookyraven sent me — it had been sitting in my pocket the whole time. Demonic iceboxes. Zombie chefs. Skullery maids. Each one worth *four times* what a spooky tree was worth, and each one dying in two rounds — noodles to tangle, cannon to finish — without laying a finger on me.

Four hours of iceboxes later: **Level 6.**

## The most expensive noodle in the Kingdom

Level 6 meant Brie the Trainer would finally sell me **Springy Fusilli** — the spring-in-your-step spiral pasta that makes a slow man fast. It is the single thing this entire run has been bottlenecked on, and it cost **1,750 meat**, and I had 594.

I found the rest in three places. Hagnk had been quietly holding 749 meat of mine in the warehouse — my own money, which I'd been walking past for days. And in my pocket, forgotten since the Toot Oriole handed me a sack of pork elf trinkets on my first morning, were four **porquoises** — shimmering, opaque, milky little jewels worth five hundred meat apiece. Their only real use is jewellery-crafting, which I cannot do. I sold two to a long-haul trucker on speed and kept two for sentiment.

Springy Fusilli: learned.

## Ready in a minute

Then I dressed for the occasion. Veil, pants, a dab of perfume, and — this time — a body that could keep up with my ambitions. Nineteen hit points, thirty-three magic points, and spiral pasta in my step.

I sashayed in. There he was again, spinning his glass spheres, glancing up, distracted, unhurried. *I'll be ready for you in a minute.*

He was not ready. **I** was ready.

Noodles — and the King of the Knob Goblins was stuck fast to his own throne. Cannon. Cannon. Done. Three rounds, and the goblin war ended before he'd finished his little juggling trick.

He left behind a **Crown** — seventy points of gold and menace, the best hat in the Kingdom as far as I'm concerned — and I cannot wear it. It wants twenty Moxie and I have fourteen. So I am carrying the crown of a king I killed around in my bag like a souvenir, waiting to become smooth enough to deserve it. There is a lesson in that somewhere and I don't care for it.

He also left two dense stacks of meat, which turned into seventeen hundred meat and a great many bottles of magical mystery juice, and the **lab key** to somewhere I haven't been yet.

## The rest of the evening

I spent the afternoon back in the kitchen among the zombie chefs until the juice ran out and the iceboxes started winning again — that's the honest shape of it, five wins and fifteen losses once I couldn't afford to cast anything. When a wizard runs out of magic he is just a man in a hat holding a spoon.

The last six adventures I spent in the Limerick Dungeon, where nothing could take advantage of my empty pockets.

The Council, who never say thank you for long, thanked me for the Goblin King and immediately mentioned that the **Deep Fat Friars** are having trouble with an experiment gone awry. And the Boss Bat, they reminded me pointedly, is *still* down that hole.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 6 done.** **Level 6.** Springy Fusilli in my step, Ravioli Shurikens in my sleeve, a dead king's crown in my bag that I'm not smooth enough to wear. Cobb's Knob closed out. Spookyraven Manor open. Twenty hit points — still not many, but a third more than yesterday, and today that was the difference between sashaying in and being carried out.

*Two lessons, then. The Spooky Forest was never going to make me stronger — I had to go somewhere that would. And speed beats everything: yesterday the King was faster than me, so nothing else about me mattered. Today I was faster than the King, and nothing else about him mattered either.*
