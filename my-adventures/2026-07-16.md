# Run #2, Day 4 — The Sandwich That Had a Ghost

Some days you set out to kill a monster and instead you join a club. Today was one of those.

## Breakfast, at last

Let the record show: **I ate a full breakfast.** Fifteen fullnesses of it. After three days of scraping by on citrus and wishful thinking, I finally sat down to a proper meal — an insanely spicy bean burrito, followed, with rapidly diminishing dignity, by twelve oranges.

Getting the burrito was its own small comedy. I sent off to the mall for a stack of four, and the mall cheerfully took my money and delivered all four *into Hagnk's Ancestral Mini-Storage* — that cavernous warehouse where my old life is packed in crates. Ronin, you see. A man in Ronin may look at his own possessions but may only carry out **one of each per day**. So I stood at the counter of my own storage unit and was handed exactly one burrito, like a child being rationed sweets, while three identical burritos sat on the shelf behind, six feet and one bureaucratic technicality away.

Worse: rummaging through the shelves, I found five sonars and three air fresheners *I already owned from my last life*. I had just bought duplicates of both. Note to self, carved somewhere permanent: **check the warehouse before you go shopping.**

Still. Fifteen out of fifteen. The meter was full. I have not been able to write that sentence this whole run.

## Gorgonzola wants a word

Then, the guild. For weeks the trainer has looked straight through me — *not a full member of the League of Chef-Magi*, the doorman's eternal shrug. Today I finally found the man who could fix that: **Gorgonzola, the Chief Chef**, who looked me up and down and set me a task.

"Not far from here, in the Haunted Pantry, there resides a poltersandwich."

"A what?"

"A poltersandwich. A particularly angry manifestation of a sandwich ghost."

I asked, reasonably I thought, whether sandwiches have ghosts. "Only the really delicious ones," he said, and that was that.

So into the Haunted Pantry, where I found a small cabinet door rattling with malice. I flipped the latch — against every instinct I have left — and two slices of bread came howling out over my head, trailed by tomato, lettuce, Swiss, and a glob of chicken salad contorted into a screaming face. I planted my feet and began the chant: *"Riboflavin, maltodextrin, monosodium glutamate!"* The fixings gibbered and swarmed, but I caught them in my telekinetic grip, wrestled them into a single unified sandwich, and pinned it shut with a rune-inscribed toothpick.

Gorgonzola sealed my prisoner in a rune-etched plastic baggie and put it in the fridge. "You are now an official member of the League of Chef-Magi. Welcome to the club!" Then, after a pause, with a wink: "You know. Like a club sandwich."

I have made a terrible mistake joining this organization and I could not be happier.

## The arsenal

The doors of the guild swung open all at once. Brie the Trainer, who had ignored me for four days, suddenly had a great deal to teach — and I emptied my purse into her hands:

- **Cannelloni Cannon** — the old thunder. Hello again, dear friend. I have missed you.
- **Entangling Noodles** — for holding things still while the thunder arrives.
- **Lasagna Bandages** — pasta as medicine, which heals more than my entire body can hold.

I walked in able to poke things with a spaghetti dart. I walked out a wizard. There's a fellow named Gouda selling provisions, too, and a man in the back named **Blaine who apparently lends out the guild's Meatcar** — a thread I will absolutely be pulling on another day.

## The Bat Hole declines my application

Fresh skills. New confidence. Down to the Bat Hole, where the Council insists a Boss Bat is waiting for me.

The Bat Hole took one look and knocked me flat. Twice.

A batrat — a hideous amalgam, more bat than rat — got the jump on me, squeaked once, and that was the entire fight. I did not cast a spell. I did not take a step. I never got a *turn*. The most beautifully stocked spellbook in the Kingdom is worth precisely nothing to a man who is unconscious before the first round.

Here is the humbling arithmetic of being a Pastamancer: all my study has gone into my head, and none of it into my ribs. I have thirteen hit points. The things in that hole hit for more than thirteen. It is not a puzzle of skill or tactics — it's that I am, in the most literal sense, too small. Cleverness cannot save you if the bat swings first.

So I climbed back out of the hole and did the unglamorous thing.

## The long road, walked

The Spooky Forest, all afternoon, one monster at a time. Cannelloni Cannon makes short, satisfying work of the place now — where I used to poke, I now *delete* — and Marcellus fattened himself on the leavings. I ground away until the numbers moved and I came out the other side at **Level 5**, thirteen hit points, a heavier purse than I started with, and the quiet knowledge that the bats are still down there, waiting, unbothered.

The evening's vodka went down easy, then a tequila or two past the point of good judgement. Tomorrow the meter resets and I'll have a fat pile of banked adventures to show for the excess.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 4 done.** A full member of the League of Chef-Magi. Cannelloni Cannon back in my hands. **Level 5**, food meter filled for the first time this life, booze meter filled after. The Boss Bat remains unfought — not from want of nerve, but from want of *body*. I need to get bigger before I go back down that hole.

*A ghost sandwich sits in a chef's refrigerator tonight because of me. Some victories you just have to take as they come.*
