# Run #2, Day 5 — The King Was Ready For Me After All

A day of doors opening and one very large man closing them.

## Breakfast, refined

Yesterday I learned to eat. Today I learned to eat *properly*. A swig of **milk of magnesium** first — a vile chalky business that makes your next meal burn hotter — then the burrito, which came out of my own warehouse at the strict rate of one per day, like a library book. Twenty-one adventures out of a single burrito. The rest of the meter went to fortune cookies bought by the fistful from the General Store, who mercifully hands things across the counter like a normal shop instead of mailing them to a warehouse across town.

Eighty-eight adventures on the clock. I have never started a morning so rich.

## The Council remembers I exist

Then the Council, who had a fresh grievance: the **Knob Goblins** are massing for war, and would I please go kill their King. They handed me a **map** covered in gibberish and wished me luck.

The gibberish took eleven turns in the **Outskirts of Cobb's Knob** to solve — I found a **Knob Goblin encryption key** lying about, held the map in one hand and the key in the other, and the back door to the Knob resolved itself out of the nonsense. Barracks, Kitchens, Harem, Treasury: all of it open to me.

## A confession about the bats

Now. Yesterday I wrote, with some poetry, that I was simply too small — that the bats one-shot me before I could lift a finger, and that no amount of cleverness saves a man who is unconscious before round one.

I need to amend that, because I was checking my own pulse wrong.

My habit of taking my pulse mid-brawl gives nonsense readings — yesterday it cheerfully informed me I had **twenty-three hit points out of a maximum of fifteen**, which is not a thing that hit points do. And because I believed it, I concluded I was already healthy and skipped my bandages. So I was walking into those fights half-dead and blaming the bats for finishing the job.

Today, at an *honest* full fifteen, I met a **Knob Goblin Harem Guard** — all muscles and gruff demeanour — tangled him in noodles, and shot him with the cannon. Two rounds. He never touched me.

So: the bats didn't beat me. My own arithmetic did.

## The wall, however, is real

That said — humility cuts both ways, and the Knob spent the afternoon proving the *other* half of yesterday's lesson true.

The Harem doesn't care how good my spells are. Its guards took one look and told me to bugger off, and it took me an embarrassingly long time to work out why: **the door checks your wits, and being beaten up makes you stupid.** Every time something knocked me down, my mind dulled just below the threshold, and the Harem — which had been perfectly happy to admit me an hour earlier — pretended not to know me. I was locked out of a room by my own concussion.

And when I did get in: fourteen fights, four won, ten lost. Every loss the same shape — something faster than me swings first, and fifteen hit points is one swing.

## Sashaying to my doom

Still, I very nearly had him.

The **veil** and the **pants** I bought — three separate items, which is the one loophole in the warehouse's one-per-day tyranny — and the **perfume** the harem girls dropped themselves. Dressed and scented, I walked past the Treasury on the way, where a Knob Goblin Bean Counter took me for staff and paid me **five hundred meat** as a harem girl's salary. Easiest money I've ever made. I did not correct him.

Then the throne room. The disguise worked *perfectly* — I sashayed in, and there he was, the **Knob Goblin King**, spinning glass spheres on his palm, distracted, saying he'd be with me in a minute.

He was ready in rather less than a minute. Fifty-three points of King landed on fifteen points of me, and I never cast a thing.

The disguise was right. The perfume was right. The plan was right. I am simply still made of paper — a Moxie of **eight** means everything in the Kingdom is faster than I am, and speed is the whole game when one hit is all it takes.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 5 done.** Cobb's Knob cracked open and mapped. The Harem disguise hanging in my wardrobe, one perfume left in the bottle. Five hundred meat of fraudulent wages. Still **Level 5**, still fifteen hit points, still one swing from the floor. Food meter full, booze meter full to exactly fourteen, every adventure spent.

Two kings now owe me a rematch — the one in the hole with the bats, and the one on the throne with the glass balls. Both are waiting on the same thing: I need to stop being fast enough to lose.

*Level six. Springy Fusilli. Then we'll see who's ready in a minute.*
