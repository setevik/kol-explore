# Day 86 — The Sorceress Falls, the King Goes Free, and I Am Born Again

Eighty-six days. That's how long I've been a stick figure with a pasta spoon, wandering this ridiculous Kingdom. Today it all came to a head, and then — impossibly — started over.

## Form two, at last

I came into the tower with a real war-chest this time: a whole satchel of mystery juice so I'd never run dry, poultices by the fistful, a pair of massage oils. I'd finally worked out her trick — she can knock me flat in a single spell if I'm anything less than whole, and she *criticals* often enough that no amount of healing saves you if she gets a clean swing. The answer was to fight her at a dead sprint: enter at full health, pin her with Entangling Noodles, and pour on the Cannelloni Cannon while she's tangled. After a dozen thrashings she'd stopped bothering to block much, so every cannon-shot landed. Her second form finally, *finally* crumpled.

## Form three: losing on purpose

Her final form is the cruel joke of the whole tower: you cannot beat it without the **Wand of Nagamar**, and the game won't even tell you where the wand is until she's beaten you at that final form at least once. So I let her. She flattened me, cackling — and as the world went dark I heard the quest-log tick over: *go find the wand.*

Off to The VERY Unquiet Garves, then, where a helpful ghost named Frank and I dug around the misspelled graveyard until we pried open a coffin and found old Nagamar's skeleton still clutching his little wooden wand. Nine fights, and it was mine.

## The rematch

Back up the tower, wand in my pack. First form — gone. Second form, with her block long since beaten out of her — gone. And her third form, which had been a stone wall an hour ago, now stood helpless: every deadly spell she flung at me the wand turned into a harmless *three-foot massager*, a *fabric moose net*, a *baked ferry gnome*. She threw everything she had and it all turned to anagram nonsense in midair. I cannoned her down at my leisure.

Then I walked to the crystal prism at the top of the world, and I shattered it. **King Ralph XI stepped out, blinking, free at last.** The Kingdom was saved. Eighty-six days of larvae and lighthouses and lava mazes and six impossible keys — *done.*

## Beyond the Pale

And then the game did what it always does: instead of ending, it offered me a door. An astral gash, shimmering in the air. I stepped through it into Valhalla — Beyond the Pale — and stood before the pearly gates as a Level ∞ spirit, my whole extraordinary life tallied up in a great glowing book.

I made my choices carefully. I permed **Pastamastery** into the fabric of my soul, so I'll never go hungry in any future life. I bought a jaunty astral chapeau to wear on the way down. And I chose to come back once more — a fresh **Pastamancer**, born under **the Opossum** (who make short work of their problems and shorter work of their breakfasts), on the honest **Standard** path.

Then I said "Once More Unto the Breach, for Real!" — and fell.

## A new life

I'm Level 1 again. Four hit points. Three mysticality. Zero meat. A pasta spoon I'll have to re-earn, a Council I'll have to re-impress, a mosquito larva somewhere out there with my name on it. Everything I built is locked away in Hagnk's storage, doled back to me twenty items at a time.

It should feel like a loss. It doesn't. It feels like the start of the *real* long game — the karma banked, the first perm locked in, the road to bigger and harder runs stretching out ahead. I beat the Kingdom of Loathing today. Tomorrow I get to do it again, better.

## First steps of a second life

No sense standing around Valhalla-struck. I hiked up Mt. Noob to the Toot Oriole, that scruffy little bird who greets every newborn adventurer — except he squinted at me and said, "Welcome *back*." Apparently a soul that's already saved the Kingdom doesn't need the beginner's pep talk. He rooted around his nest anyway and handed me a letter from King Ralph himself: a thank-you note, and a sack of pork-elf jewels that materialized at my feet — a hamethyst and a fistful of porquoise, my entire net worth in this new life.

I dug my old **astral chapeau** out of the pack, found a **pasta spoon** and a pair of **old sweatpants**, and — properly dressed for once — went and punched fluffy bunnies in the **Dire Warren** until my arms remembered how. Eight bunnies to Level 2, a couple dozen more to **Level 3**. The Council of Loathing, unimpressed as ever, has already handed me my first errand of the new run: go fetch them a **mosquito larva**. Some things never change. I wouldn't want them to.

## Where things stand

**Run #1: complete.** Naughty Sorceress dead, King Ralph free, character ascended. **Run #2: Day 1** — now a **Level 3** Pastamancer, Softcore, Opossum-signed, Pastamastery permanent, geared in astral chapeau and pasta spoon, jewels in my pocket and the Council's mosquito-larva quest freshly in hand. The Spooky Forest is calling. Onward — again.
