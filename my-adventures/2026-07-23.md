# Run #2, Day 11 — Level Eight, and a Cyrpt Too Deep

A book, a level, and a hole in the ground I can only chip at.

## The book I nearly sold

The day began with me broke — thirty-four meat to my name after yesterday's juice-buying — and rummaging through my possessions for anything to pawn. Near the top of the pile sat something called the **Necrotelicomnicon**, worth two thousand meat to the right buyer, and I very nearly sold it on the spot.

Then I read the label. It's a spellbook, and it's *mine* in the specific way few things are: **+10 to the damage of every spell I cast**, and it tunes my pasta to Spooky besides. I'd have sold my own right arm for pocket change. Instead I tucked it under my off-hand — I had to set down the pool cue to do it, but the cue's only for hustling ghosts, and I do my real work with a spoon and a book now.

## Level Eight

Then, the kitchen. Always the kitchen. Forty-nine wins out of fifty-four in the first long sitting, then a few more, and somewhere in the middle of a zombie chef the numbers ticked over: **Level Eight.**

The Council, who have ignored me for two days on the grounds that I wasn't experienced enough to bother, suddenly had work again. An **aura of extreme Spookiness** is leaking out of the **Cyrpt** by the Misspelled Cemetary, some horror stirring up the dead. They handed me an **Evilometer** — a little device that reads how much wickedness is left in each corner of the place — and sent me down.

## The Cyrpt takes my measure

The Evilometer read two hundred, near enough: fifty units of evil in each of four defiled chambers, and every unit is a monster I have to put down.

The Friars, sensibly, offer travellers a blessing before such errands, and Brother Smothers rubbed ashes on my face that ward off Spooky for a while. Blessed and buffed and full of magic, I went into the **Nook** and killed a spiny skeleton in four clean rounds without it touching me. *This*, I thought, *is fine.*

It was fine for about twenty minutes.

The Cyrpt's dead hit in the fifties and seventies — far above anything in the manor — and they only fall while I have magic to spend and ash on my face. When the mystery juice ran dry, and then the blessing quietly wore off, I stopped noticing until I looked up and found I'd lost twenty-three of my last thirty fights, standing at a skeleton with empty hands and an unwarded face, taking the full brunt of the grave.

I chipped the Nook down from fifty to forty-one and no further. There are one hundred and ninety-one units of evil still down there. That's not an afternoon; that's most of a week.

## Knowing when to bank

So I did the sensible, unglamorous thing I keep having to relearn: I stopped throwing myself at the wall.

I limped back up to the kitchen — no juice, no blessing, just a spoon — and found that the humble zombie chefs still die to a plain spaghetti dart at no cost to my magic at all. **Twenty-four wins, one loss.** I spent the last of the day banking safe wisdom instead of donating my hit points to the undead, and came out the other side at Level Eight and change, a little closer to the strength the Cyrpt actually demands.

The nightcap, as ever now, done by the numbers: mixed a spritzer for free, drank to exactly fourteen, and one vodka martini over the line for six adventures I'll wake up with.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 11 done.** **Level 8.** A proper spellbook under my arm, the Cyrpt quest open, and a grim tally on the Evilometer that I've barely dented. Two hundred meat, no juice, and a clear understanding that the Cyrpt is a wall I get over by being *bigger*, not braver.

*The dead have been down there a long time. They can wait a few more days while I grow into the job.*
