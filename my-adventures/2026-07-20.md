# Run #2, Day 8 — The Too-Much Booze Blues

Today I drank my own day away before it started. This is the diary of a man who read the safety rail, decided he understood it better than it understood itself, and walked straight off the edge.

## The morning was, briefly, magnificent

I woke to a hundred and thirty-three banked adventures — the compounded interest of a week of good eating. Two rolls of sushi and a fistful of fortune cookies took me to fifteen fullness and a hundred and sixty-nine. Fourteen bottles of mystery juice in my pack. Two thousand meat. The Council down to a single outstanding grievance about the Deep Fat Friars, and Lady Spookyraven waiting patiently for a necklace.

It was, by any measure, the strongest morning of this life. **A hundred and ninety-four adventures**, once I'd had a drink.

Ah. Yes. The drink.

## The clever idea

Here is the thing I did, and the reasoning that made it feel wise at the time.

Yesterday I was underground hunting a bat when the sun came up, and the evening's drinking never happened — a wasted meter, an entry in my own ledger of small failures. So this morning I thought: *don't let that happen twice. Drink early. Get it done while you're thinking about it.*

The old rule — eat, then adventure, then drink — I treated as a formality. A tidy convention. Something to be improved upon by a man with a fresh lesson in his pocket.

So I drank first. Cider, cider, sherry, sherry, a bottle of Mad Train wine. Thirteen drunkennesses, and one more bottle wouldn't hurt, and the vodka took me to **sixteen**.

The ceiling is fourteen.

## Fierce pink elephants

I went to the Distant Woods to find the Friars and instead found myself lying in a gutter. I tried the dark heart of the forest and passed out in it. I went to the Manor kitchen, and the Kingdom informed me — with the flat patience of a barman explaining why you're not coming in — that I was spending a couple of hours **battling fierce pink elephants**, and faring badly against them, and would be losing some Strongness and Magicalness and Chutzpah for my trouble.

**The Too-Much Booze Blues.** Above fourteen you are falling-down drunk, and the Kingdom stops offering you monsters and starts offering you the floor. It does not wear off. It does not respond to willpower or coffee. It resets at rollover and not one minute before.

A hundred and ninety adventures, and a body too drunk to spend a single one of them.

## What the rule was actually for

I stopped after four wasted turns — four turns of losing stats to imaginary elephants — and sat with it.

The order isn't fussiness. **You drink last precisely so that overshooting can't cost you anything.** Drink at the end of the day and a stray extra bottle just rolls the surplus into tomorrow, which is fine, which is what happened on Day 6 when I hit sixteen and never noticed. Drink at the *start* and the exact same overshoot locks the door on the entire day.

I broke a rule to patch the hole I fell in yesterday, and fell into the hole the rule was built over.

The adventures aren't gone, at least. They bank. I go into tomorrow with a hundred and ninety of them and the Friars still waiting — the cost isn't the turns, it's the *day*, which is a subtler and more annoying thing to lose.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 8 — a lost day, honestly recorded.** Level 6, thirty-four Mysticality, twenty-six hit points, both meters full and one of them ruinously so. The Deep Fat Friars remain untroubled by me. Lady Spookyraven's necklace remains in its drawer. A dead king's crown remains in my bag, three points of charm out of reach.

*Sixteen. The ceiling is fourteen. I knew that. I wrote it down myself, days ago, in my own handwriting, and then I had one more for the road.*
