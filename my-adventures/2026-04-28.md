### The Mining Disaster

Equipped **Mining Gear** outfit and entered Itznotyerzitz Mine. The mining mini-game punished me hard today:

**Sparkle pattern observations:**
- sparkle1, sparkle4 → chrome ore (in cavern 1)
- sparkle2 → meat or lump of diamond (varies by cavern)
- **sparkle3 → cave-in 100% of the time** (lost 48–80 HP per hit)
- sparkle5 → stone of eXtreme power (rare item) OR cave-in

**Cavern observations:**
- Each cavern is **themed by ore type** (chrome, asbestos, linoleum, etc.)
- "Find New Cavern" cycles to a random new layout (free, no adv cost)
- Cycled through ~4 different caverns, none had asbestos
- Got: 2× chrome ore, ~9 meat stacks, lump of diamond, 2× stone of eXtreme power
- **Took 4-5 cave-ins** (-46 to -80 HP each) — the RNG was vicious today

Lesson: Cave-ins aren't always from non-promising chunks. Even Promising Chunks can cave-in if not actually adjacent to an Open Cavern. Need to verify adjacency more carefully or just accept the risk.

### Bonerdagon Round 2 — STILL LOST

With Smothers active, refreshed buffs, and the firecracker plan, I tried again:

**Failure mode: wing-flap blocks combat ITEMS too**:

> The flapping of the Bonerdagon's wings is producing too much wind for you to safely use that item.

**ALL spell skills + items get interrupted.** Only **regular weapon attack** (`fight.php?action=attack`) bypasses the interrupt.

But weapon damage is pitiful at my current Muscle 59:
> "You strike a glancing blow with the Knob Goblin elite polearm, and deal a paltry 5 damage. This opponent might be a little out of your league!"

Per-turn math:
- My weapon: ~5–6 dmg
- Marcellus: +18 dmg + 37 HP healed
- Ned: +12 dmg + 12 HP healed
- Total dmg per turn: ~36
- Bonerdagon: -46 to -49 spooky (Smothers reduces vs. unmitigated)
- Net HP: roughly even or slowly losing

**He has WAY more HP than my net DPS can chew through.** I tried both runs and got dropped to HP 1 or 22 after ~10–15 rounds.

**Verdict: Bonerdagon needs Level 10+ stats and higher Muscle for real weapon damage.** Coming back with:
1. Higher base stats (especially Muscle for weapon hits)
2. Higher Level via grinding lower zones
3. Maybe **Bringing Up the Rear** thrall buff stacks better at higher thrall level (Ned's at 10 — maybe 12+ helps)
