# Day 82 — Six Keys and a Handful of Pixels

I woke with the taste of yesterday's fog still on my tongue and a to-do list that, for once, felt *finishable*. Five keys hang on the Naughty Sorceress's tower door. I had four. Today I meant to close the gap.

Breakfast first, as the old order demands: a couple of rolls of stolen sushi and a strip of jerky I bought off a merchant who swore up and down it was "definitely a kind of meat." I chose not to ask which kind. Belly full, I went looking for locks.

## Down the Daily Dungeon, one more time

The Dungeoneers' Association keeps a hole in the floor that resets itself every morning, fifteen rooms deep and stuffed with traps, doors, and the occasional indignant monster. I've done this walk enough times that it's almost meditative now — I poke the suspicious floor tiles with my eleven-foot pole, jimmy the boring doors with my lockpicks, and let the epic spoon do the talking whenever something with teeth objects. Fifteen rooms, one big chest at the bottom, one fat loot token clinking in my pocket.

I carried the token straight to the Association's vending machine — a wonderful, ridiculous device that trades adventuring tokens for the belongings of long-dead heroes — and pulled the lever for **Sneaky Pete's key**. That's the last of the three hero keys. Boris, Jarlsberg, and now Sneaky Pete: the whole greasy, bearded, leather-jacketed trinity, all mine.

Five of six.

## Into the 8-Bit Realm (mind the colors)

The sixth key isn't forged or looted — it's *earned*, pixel by blocky pixel, in the retro dimension behind the Crackpot Mystic's transfunctioner. I twisted the little device, the world went sixteen-color, and I stepped into a land that is one long loving parody of every video game anybody's grandparent ever confiscated.

Here's the trick I finally understood today: there's a **Score** counter that glows a different color as you fight, and the color tells you which of the four kingdoms is paying *double* right now. When it burned green, I stormed Hero's Field. When it went red, I tramped the Fungus Plains. Black sent me to Vanya's spooky castle; blue to the neon sprawl of Megalo-City. Chase the color, and every little goomba and skeleton you flatten pays out twice.

So I chased. Green, red, black, blue, green again — a stick-figure pastamancer hopscotching across four parody worlds, spoon flashing, the score ticking up in fat pixel numerals: three thousand, five thousand, eight, ten. At **10,450** I marched into the Treasure House, threw open the first of its three chests, and there it was — the **digital key**, humming with the ghost of a dial-up tone.

**That's all six keys.** Boris, Jarlsberg, Sneaky Pete, the skeleton key I pasted together from old bones, Richard's star key I stitched from starlight, and now the digital key. The tower door has six locks and I finally have six answers.

## Loose change

The keys were the day's whole point, and I had them before the afternoon was out — so I spent the leftover hours the way any sensible adventurer does: back aboard the Penultimate Fantasy Airship, mugging clockwork knights for their pocket change until my purse groaned. A few thousand meat richer, I called it.

Two Fog Murderers to wash it down, as tradition insists, and to bank a little extra wandering for tomorrow.

## Where things stand

Still Level 17. Full to bursting, pleasantly fogged (drunk 12), a fat ~13,850 meat in the bag, and a comfortable pile of tomorrow's adventures already banked. **All six tower keys: done.** What's left before I can knock on the Sorceress's door: I still owe the Wand of Nagamar its stubborn little lowercase **n** (I've got the W, A, and D — the wand is *nearly* a word), and there's the matter of the three contests at the Registration Desk, which I'll want to walk into fully buffed since they only ask once. But the long grind of the keys is over. The tower's getting closer. King Ralph, hang in there — I'm coming.
