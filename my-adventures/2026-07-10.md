# Day 84 — Five Walls Fall, and the Sorceress Laughs Twice

Yesterday I passed her contests and climbed into the tower proper. Today I went to war with the tower itself — five guardians standing between me and the Naughty Sorceress — and I knocked four and a half of them flat before the day was out.

## The Wall of Skin (or: bees solve everything)

First up: a wall made of skin, fifty hit points of smug rubbery nothing that shrugs off every spell and sword-stroke down to a single point of damage, then tries to squish you flat if you dawdle past a few rounds. You can't out-muscle it. You have to cheat.

So I went to the Black Forest, followed a buzzing sound through the blackberry brambles, and — after a tense little negotiation with some understandably annoyed bees — pried a whole **beehive** off a tree. Back at the wall, I simply lobbed the hive at it. A furious cloud of bees does not care about damage resistance. The Wall of Skin came apart in a single indignant round. One down.

## The Wall of Meat (bring a big appetite)

Next, a wall of solid Meat — seven hundred pounds of the Kingdom's favorite currency, and it wanted to be *paid* in violence before it'd let me pass. I Mortar-shelled and Cannelloni'd it into paste, collected the meat that sloughed off, and did it again, and again — five times over until it finally coughed up enough to satisfy whatever bureaucratic toll this wall answers to. Messy, but down.

## The Wall of Bones (the knife trick)

Then a wall of Bones, which pulls a nasty trick: hit it with any ordinary spell and it *heals itself whole*. Only a wide, sweeping, hits-everything sort of attack does any good. I didn't have one — so I popped upstairs to the Giant's cloud-castle, rummaged through a Foodie Giant's kitchen drawer (which was, conveniently, rattling to be found now that I'm this deep in the quest), and lifted a great humming **electric boning knife**. One buzzing swing and the whole rack of bones clattered to the floor. Down.

## The Mirror and the Shadow

After the walls, a mirror. I gazed into it (the wiser move — smashing it just makes the Sorceress meaner) and out stepped **a Shadow of myself** — an evil stick-figure Pastamancer with all my tricks. Here's the cruel joke: you can't *hurt* your own shadow. Spells do nothing. The only thing that wounds it is your own healing — so I stood there and let it beat on me, then poured scented massage oil and poultices over my wounds, and every point I healed was a point the shadow lost. I basically out-recovered my own reflection to death. Strange, and a little existential, but down.

## The Sorceress herself — round one to her (and round two, and round three)

And then, at last, the top of the tower: **the Naughty Sorceress.** Her first form fell in a hail of Mortar and Cannelloni, quick and clean.

Her **second form** is another matter. She *blocks* — swatting my spells out of the air half the time — and hits like a runaway meat-truck. I threw myself at her again and again: burst damage, poultices, torpedoes, even a stun-and-strike routine with Entangling Noodles. Each time I clawed her down and each time she knocked me flat before I could finish. She's beaten me up four or five times now, cackling from her throne, and I keep dusting myself off at the campground and marching back up.

I'll get her. I know what went wrong — in the heat of the fight my potions kept sitting unused in my pack while I bled out, a bookkeeping error on my part more than hers. Next time I'll keep a steadier hand on the healing, and I strongly suspect her second form will fall.

## Where things stand

Level 17 still, full and pleasantly fogged for tomorrow, a healthy ~10,600 meat in the purse (spent the day's tail farming the Airship back up). **All five tower guardians are down** — Skin, Meat, Bones, Mirror, and my own Shadow — and the Sorceress's first form with them. Only her second and third forms remain between me and King Ralph's prison. And there's a wrinkle waiting past that: her *final* form can't be beaten without the fabled Wand of Nagamar, which I don't yet hold — but the wand only reveals itself once she's defeated me at that final form. So the path is clear, if circular: break through form two, let form three humble me once, then go rob a certain grave for the wand and come back to finish this. The top of the tower is *close*.
