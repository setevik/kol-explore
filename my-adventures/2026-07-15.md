# Run #2, Day 3 — The Baron in the Cellar

Third day back on the wheel. A light breakfast of citrus (my fresh little stomach still can't handle the good stuff — tried a roll of stolen sushi and it just sort of… declined to be eaten, too rich for a Level 4 palate), then off to deal with the Typical Tavern's eternal, inevitable rat problem.

## Down the dark stairs

Ear'l Ender the bartender waved me toward the cellar with the weary air of a man who has had this exact conversation a hundred times. "Rat problem, y'see." I see, Ear'l. I always see.

Down I went into a pitch-black five-by-five grid of *maybe-rats*, and I made — I'll be honest — a proper hash of it at first. There's a dripping **rat faucet** down there, the actual source of the vermin, and every time I gave it a hopeful twist a fat drunk rat squirted out onto the floor like the world's worst gumball machine. I must have wrung that tap a dozen times, drowning in rats of my own making, before I remembered how this actually works: you *leave the stupid faucet alone*, and you go looking for the rat who owns the place.

## His Lordship

And own it he does. In a tiny mansion tucked in a far corner of the cellar sat **Baron von Ratsworth** — a rat of breeding, of means, of truly punchable smugness. Marcellus and I cornered him, and after a flurry of spaghetti-darts the Baron met the same fate as his subjects. He left behind his **monocle**, which I promptly screwed into my own eye socket. It magnifies loot wonderfully and makes me look like I know what I'm doing. (I remember this monocle. I wore it for eighty-odd days last life. Hello again, old friend.)

With the Baron dealt with, I climbed back up, told Ear'l the faucet was sorted, and he grunted something about a ditzy barmaid named Luanne leaving it running. Quest closed. The Council, predictably, already has my next assignment: something is amiss in the **Bat Hole**, and there's a **Boss Bat** with my name on it.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 3 done.** Still Level 4 (a whisker from 5), HP creeping up to 11, Marcellus fattening nicely, a Baron's monocle over one eye and ~1,800 meat in pocket. **Typical Tavern rat problem: solved.** Twelve drunkennesses of cheap vodka to see out the evening. Next: into the Bat Hole. The old road home, one smug rodent aristocrat at a time.
