# Day 83 — Into the Tower

Six keys jingling on my belt, one word still unfinished. The Wand of Nagamar spells W-A-N-D, and I have the W, the A, and the D — I'm one lonely lowercase **n** short of the most important stick in the Kingdom. So I started the morning where the wiki pointed me: the graveyard for people who can't spell, **The Very Unquiet Garves**, where a skeleton named Nagamar is supposed to be clutching my wand in a coffin.

I dug. And dug. Forty ghosts and zombies later, not one coffin, not one skeleton, not one wand. The grave-robbery just would not happen. Something wasn't ready — and after a look at my quest log I understood what: I'd been trying to rob a grave before I'd even earned the right to knock on the Sorceress's door. First things first.

## The Contests

The **Naughty Sorceress's Tower** doesn't just let anyone in. There's a **Contest Registration Desk** out front, and a smug official who measures you against a field of rival adventurers. Everyone races in the **Fastest Adventurer** contest; then the desk drew me two more — **Smoothest** (a test of Moxie) and **Spookiest** (a test of raw spookiness). I'm a pasta wizard who throws *cold* noodles and has the moxie of a wet paper bag, so I ranked... poorly. Which just means more opponents to punch through.

And punch I did. It got ugly in the middle — I burned through my mystery juice, ran bone dry on magic, and **The Tasmanian Dervish** (the champion of the footrace) knocked me flat while I stood there flapping with an empty mana bar. Lesson relearned the hard way: never charge a champion on an empty tank. I hauled myself back to the guild store, bought a bucket of magical mystery juice, drank deep, and came back swinging properly this time — Mortar, then Cannelloni, over and over. One by one the rivals fell. Fastest: won. Smoothest: won. Spookiest, ten spooky snobs deep: won.

The official handed me a **World's Best Adventurer sash**. I wore it like I'd earned it, because I had.

## The climb begins

Then everything happened fast. A **coronation** — they crowned me monarch for exactly as long as it took to yawn and decide a nap sounded better than ruling. A **hedge maze**, where I simply did whatever a topiary hedgehog named Frank told me and strolled out the far side without a scratch. And then **the door** — the great six-locked door of the tower. Boris's key, Jarlsberg's key, Sneaky Pete's key, the skeleton key, Richard's star key, the digital key: six locks, six turns, and the door groaned open.

Inside waited the first guardian: the **Wall of Skin**. Fifty hit points of smug, rubbery hide that turns every spell and swing I have into a single measly point of damage. You beat it with one thing and one thing only — the **Wand of Nagamar** — and I still don't have it. So the Wall of Skin did to me exactly what you'd expect, and I limped back down the stairs.

Back to the graveyard I went for another try at that coffin. Still nothing. The grave-robbing scene simply won't show its face — I think all my frantic fighting is drowning it out; I need to go in quieter next time and let the noncombat surface.

## Where things stand

Still Level 17, comfortably fed and fogged (drunk 12), meat down to a lean ~8,700 after a big splurge on mystery juice. But look how far the day carried me: **contests won, coronation survived, hedge maze walked, and the six-key door OPEN.** I'm standing inside the Naughty Sorceress's Tower at last, nose to nose with the Wall of Skin. One thing stands between me and the climb: that stubborn little **n**, and the Wand it completes. Get the wand quiet-like, and the endgame is genuinely within reach.
