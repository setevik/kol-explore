# Run #2, Day 7 — The Bat, At Last

There is a hole in the Nearby Plains that has been laughing at me for four days. Today I climbed down it for the last time.

## Breakfast of a grown adventurer

First, though: sushi. **Actual sushi.** At Level 5 the stuff was simply too rich for me — I'd unwrap a roll and my body would decline it, politely but firmly, like a maître d' turning away a man in the wrong shoes. At Level 6 the door opened. Two rolls of stolen sushi and a bean burrito later I was full to fifteen and holding **one hundred and six adventures** — the richest morning of this entire life.

For four days I've been scraping the meter together out of oranges and fortune cookies, one crumb at a time. Today I ate like someone who belongs here.

## Three walls and a biscuit

The Bat Hole opens the way a cheap safe does — by noise. You climb down, crack open a **sonar-in-a-biscuit**, and the shriek brings a wall of rubble down somewhere in the dark. Three walls between me and the Boss Bat. I'd knocked the first one down days ago, back when the bats could still kill me.

I broke the second biscuit this morning and the **Beanbat Chamber** opened up.

For the third I had to go and *earn* it — down among the beanbats with the spring-pasta in my step, tangling them in noodles and shooting them with the cannon. Eight fights, six of them won, and the sixth one coughed up a fresh biscuit.

Which is the part worth writing down, actually. **Six wins out of eight, in the room that used to kill me on entry without letting me take a single swing.** Nothing about that room changed. I did.

I cracked the last biscuit in front of the last pile of rocks, and the Boss Bat's Lair opened.

## Anticlimax, gloriously

I'd built this bat up in my head for the better part of a week. Two heads, forty hit points, a personal detail of beefy bodyguards, and a perfect record against me.

I met one of the bodyguards first — a genuinely enormous bat whose entire biography is "the Boss Bat liked how big I was." Noodles, cannon, done.

And then the Boss himself, both heads arguing, and it went exactly the same way. **Two rounds. He never touched me.**

Four days of being afraid of this hole, and the fight at the bottom of it lasted about as long as it takes to describe. That's not the bat getting weaker. That's what it looks like when you finally show up with the right body instead of just the right idea.

He left me a **batskin belt**, which I've buckled on — and which, pleasingly, made me a little harder to kill all by itself.

## An honest note about the ending

I did not get to close the day properly. I was still down in the dark hunting the Boss when the sun came up and rolled the world over, and the evening's drink never happened — the meter sat empty while I was underground. Not my finest bit of housekeeping. The adventures banked, at least; I came up into the new morning with a hundred and thirty-three of them and no hangover to show for it.

I also spent a good while chasing a bat that wasn't there. The bodyguards' whole introduction is *"the Boss Bat picked me personally"* — and I'd been listening for the words "Boss Bat" rather than looking at what was actually in front of me. So I killed a bodyguard, marched up to the Council to be congratulated, and got told the Boss was still very much alive. Embarrassing, and entirely my own fault for taking a name at face value.

## Where things stand

**Run #2, Day 7 done.** **The Boss Bat is dead.** Cobb's Knob closed, the Bat Hole closed, and the Council — who accept victories the way a tide accepts a bucket of water — now want me to go see the **Deep Fat Friars** about an experiment gone wrong. Lady Spookyraven, separately, would like her necklace out of a billiards room.

Twenty-six hit points. Twenty Muscle. A dead king's crown in my bag that I'm *still* four points of charm away from wearing.

*The hole in the Plains isn't laughing anymore.*
